Mailvah vs. Skrapp.io

Browser extensions are
fragile toys.

Skrapp relies on your local browser and IP address to slowly scrape social networks. It is a manual tool for SDRs. Mailvah is an automated, server-side outbound pipeline powered by our V4.5 Cluster. Extract, verify, and secure leads without clicking a single profile.

Client-Side vs. Server-Side

When you use a browser extension like Skrapp, you are doing the heavy lifting. You load the page, the extension reads the HTML, and it tries to guess the email. It is slow, prone to getting your LinkedIn account restricted, and relies entirely on your local internet connection.

Mailvah's Extraction Network operates entirely server-side. You feed the Command Center your target parameters (Job Title, Location, Industry), and our global nodes hunt the data, routing it seamlessly through our V3.2 verification engine before it ever reaches you.

Distributed Extraction Nodes

To prevent IP bans and throttling, Mailvah executes extraction and verification requests across a distributed network of bare-metal servers. From our primary hub in Dallas to edge nodes expanding across Luxembourg, Asia, South America, and Africa, your data is processed at maximum velocity.
